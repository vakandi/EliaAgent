# Leadership Wisdom Skill

## Description
Build teams that execute, culture that compounds, and leadership that scales. This skill covers Alex Hormozi's frameworks on enforcement, culture building, productivity through elimination, and why being willing to look cringe is prerequisite to growth.

## Core Principles

### 1. Rules Without Enforcement Are Suggestions
> "Rules without enforcement are not rules, they're suggestions."

Every policy you announce but don't enforce teaches your team that your words don't matter.

### 2. The Demonstration Loop
> "Define exactly what you want into observable terms, demonstrate it, get them to do it, correct immediately, give kudos publicly."

Leadership is 80% repetition of simple things done consistently.

### 3. Culture = Public Recognition
> "When culture fits start giving kudos to each other publicly, that's when you've built culture."

Culture isn't values on a wall. It's what gets celebrated when no one's watching.

### 4. Productivity Through Elimination
> "Productivity comes from all the things you choose not to do."

Your capacity is finite. Every "yes" to something is a "no" to something more important.

### 5. Losers Stay Losers Because They Fear Losing
> "Losers stay losers because they are afraid of losing, so they never try."

The risk of inaction almost always exceeds the risk of action.

### 6. Embrace the Cringe
> "Be willing to look cringe - it's a prerequisite of doing something new that you will suck."

Every expert was once a beginner. The willingness to look foolish is the price of admission to growth.

## Actionable Frameworks

### Framework 1: The Demonstration Loop (For Training Anyone)

```
STEP 1: DEFINE (Observable terms)
"What does great look like?"
- Specific behavior, not vague quality
- Measurable outcomes
- Clear examples

STEP 2: DEMONSTRATE (Show, don't tell)
"Let me show you how I do it."
- Do it at full speed, then slow
- Narrate your thinking
- Show mistakes and corrections

STEP 3: GET THEM TO DO IT
"Your turn. I'll watch."
- Have them replicate exactly
- Don't let them improvise yet
- Be patient with the process

STEP 4: CORRECT IMMEDIATELY
"[Specific feedback], like this."
- Correct in the moment
- Don't let errors compound
- Be direct, not harsh

STEP 5: GIVE KUDOS PUBLICLY
"[Name] did a great job on [specific thing]."
- Public recognition reinforces behavior
- Others learn from watching recognition
- Creates aspirational models
```

### Framework 2: The Rule Enforcement Protocol

```
BEFORE ANNOUNCING A RULE, ASK:
1. Am I willing to enforce this 100% of the time?
2. Can I clearly identify violations?
3. Do I have a consequence ready?

IF NO TO ANY → DON'T ANNOUNCE THE RULE

WHEN ENFORCING:
- Address immediately (within 24 hours)
- Be consistent (no favorites)
- Apply to yourself first
- Document the conversation

CONSEQUENCE HIERARCHY:
1st Violation: Private warning (documented)
2nd Violation: Public warning
3rd Violation: Formal consequence
4th Violation: Termination consideration

KEY: Once you enforce, follow through. Every broken promise destroys credibility.
```

### Framework 3: The Public Kudos System

```
DAILY:
- Catch someone doing something right
- Mention it in standup/public channel
- Be specific: "X did Y, which resulted in Z"

WEEKLY:
- Team shoutout in company update
- Spotlight the behavior you want replicated
- Connect action to company values

MONTHLY:
- Formal recognition program
- Peer-to-peer recognition (team votes)
- Celebrate publicly, reward privately

CULTURE FIT INDICATOR:
When team members start giving EACH OTHER kudos without prompting → Culture is working.
```

## Scripts & Templates

### Script 1: The Performance Conversation

```
"[Name], I want to talk about your performance.

Here's what I'm seeing: [Specific observation]

The impact of this is: [Measurable outcome]

Here's what great looks like: [Specific example]

I want to support you in getting there. What do you need from me?

[Listen]

Here's what I'm going to do: [Your commitment]

Here's what I need from you: [Their commitment]

Let's check in on [date] to see how it's going.

I believe you can do this. Are you in?"

EXPECTED RESPONSE: Commitment and next steps
```

### Script 2: The Public Recognition Script

```
"Before we get started, I want to recognize [Name] for [specific achievement].

What happened was: [Story of what they did]

The impact was: [Measurable result]

This is exactly the kind of [behavior/value] we want to see more of.

[Name], stand up. You crushed it."

[If in person: Applause from team]
[If remote: Emoji reactions, chat appreciation]

"Anyone else want to give a shoutout?"
```

### Script 3: The "You're Not Meeting Expectations" Talk

```
"[Name], I need to have a direct conversation with you.

[Specific expectation that isn't being met]

I've noticed [observable behavior] when I expected [expected behavior].

This has resulted in [impact].

I want to give you the chance to turn this around. Here's the plan:

[Specific, measurable improvements required]
[Timeline: by when]
[Consequences if not met]

I'm invested in your success here. I wouldn't be having this conversation if I didn't think you could meet these expectations.

Do you understand what's being asked of you?

Are you committed to this plan?"

GET VERBAL COMMITMENT. If you don't get it, escalate.
```

### Script 4: The Delegation Script

```
"I'm going to hand this off to you. Here's what success looks like:

[Specific outcome expected]

Here's how I've done it before: [Process/framework]

Here are the guardrails: [What's non-negotiable]

Here are the boundaries: [What they own vs. what I own]

I'll be available for questions, but this is yours to own.

Check in with me on [date] with [specific update].

I trust you to handle this. Let's see what you do."

FOLLOW UP: Be available but don't hover. Let them own it.
```

## Real Examples

### Example 1: The Rule That Wasn't a Rule
A startup CEO announced "we value work-life balance" but answered emails at 11pm. Team saw the contradiction. Culture became "we say one thing, do another." Then he started enforcing boundaries: no Slack after 7pm, no meetings on Fridays. Output increased. The rule became real because he enforced it on himself first.

### Example 2: The Public Kudos Multiplier
A sales team had 20% quota attainment. Started daily public recognition for small wins. Within 60 days: 73% quota attainment. The recognition created aspirational behavior. Others wanted to be "the person who gets called out for crushing it."

### Example 3: The Demonstration Loop in Action
A founder hired a "senior" developer who couldn't code. Realized he'd never actually seen the person work. Implemented: Watch them do the job before hiring. Changed process: Trial period with paid project before full offer. Bad hires dropped to zero. Good hires now include demonstration of skill, not just resume.

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

1. **Don't announce rules you won't enforce** - Every exception teaches that your words are meaningless.

2. **Don't give feedback in public that should be private** - Correct in private, praise in public.

3. **Don't let violations slide because you're tired** - Consistency is harder than the initial decision.

4. **Don't delegate without clear ownership** - "Shared responsibility" means no responsibility.

5. **Don't hover after delegating** - micromanagement destroys trust and growth.

6. **Don't skip the "correction immediately" step** - Delayed feedback loses impact and lets errors compound.

7. **Don't make culture about values on a wall** - Make it about what gets celebrated and what gets corrected.
