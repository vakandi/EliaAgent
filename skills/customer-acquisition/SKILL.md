# Customer Acquisition Skill

## Description
Stop wasting money on the wrong leads and wrong processes. This skill reveals Alex Hormozi's frameworks on cold vs. warm acquisition, response time optimization, and why "getting good at content makes ads easy." Learn to fill your pipeline efficiently.

## Core Principles

### 1. Warm Processes Kill Cold Leads
> "One of the biggest mistakes: using warm process on cold leads. The reverse always works."

Warm processes are for people who already know you. Cold processes convert cold traffic. Don't mix them.

### 2. Cold Processes Work on Warm Leads
> "If you use warm process on cold leads, you will only sell the six inch putts."

Warm processes miss all the buyers who need more convincing. Cold processes convert warm leads at higher rates.

### 3. If You Have Cold Process, You Can Use It Everywhere
> "If you have cold acquisition process, you can put warm leads through it and they buy at higher rates."

A good cold process is more valuable than a warm one because it scales.

### 4. Content Before Ads
> "Get good at content, ads are easy."

If content doesn't work organically, ads won't either. Prove the message with content first.

### 5. Response Time Is Everything
> "HubSpot did a study - contacting leads 5 minutes vs 30 minutes after opt-in = 100x more likely to connect."

Every minute counts. Speed to lead is the biggest lever in acquisition.

### 6. The 5-Minute Rule
> "5 minutes vs 30 minutes = 100x contact rate."

Automate the first response. Human touch comes after. AI can respond in seconds; humans can't.

## Actionable Frameworks

### Framework 1: The Cold vs. Warm Process Decision Tree

```
STEP 1: HOW DID THE LEAD FIND YOU?
├── Cold (Ad, cold outreach, cold content)
│   └── USE COLD PROCESS
├── Warm (Referral, organic content, existing audience)
│   └── USE WARM PROCESS
└── Very Warm (Inbound inquiry, demo request)
    └── USE HIGH-TOUCH WARM PROCESS

STEP 2: WHAT'S YOUR PROCESS?

COLD PROCESS (Direct value, no relationship assumed):
- Direct problem statement
- Clear offer
- Price transparency
- Quick commitment path
- Follow-up sequence

WARM PROCESS (Relationship leverage, softener needed):
- Reference the connection
- Build rapport first
- Ask questions
- Custom solution framing
- Longer decision timeline

STEP 3: TEST THE REVERSE
Can your cold process work on warm leads?
YES → Scale it
NO → Why not? Make it more direct.
```

### Framework 2: The Speed-to-Lead System

```
MINUTE 0-30 SECONDS:
[ ] AI/automated response triggered
[ ] Lead information captured
[ ] Routing rules applied
[ ] Next steps queued

MINUTES 1-5:
[ ] Personalized human response (high-value leads)
[ ] Calendar link included
[ ] Specific value proposition referenced
[ ] Social proof attached

HOURS 1-24:
[ ] Follow-up sequence if no response
[ ] Value-first content (not more pitching)
[ ] Alternative contact attempt
[ ] Log interaction

DAYS 2-7:
[ ] Break up sequence with value
[ ] Case study delivery
[ ] Urgency/scarcity if applicable
[ ] Final offer or exit

AUTOMATION CHECKLIST:
[ ] Email auto-response (within 30 seconds)
[ ] SMS auto-response (within 60 seconds)
[ ] CRM lead scoring
[ ] Task creation for human follow-up
[ ] Sequence management
```

### Framework 3: The Content-to-Ad Pipeline

```
STEP 1: CREATE ORGANIC CONTENT
- Identify what your ideal customer searches for
- Create 20 pieces around those topics
- Post consistently for 60 days
- Track engagement patterns

STEP 2: IDENTIFY WINNERS
- Which content gets saves, shares, comments?
- Which drives profile visits?
- Which generates DMs/messages?
- Which gets saves more than likes? (High intent signal)

STEP 3: TURN WINNERS INTO ADS
- Repurpose top organic content as ads
- Use exact messaging that resonated
- Target lookalike audiences
- Scale what works

STEP 4: REPEAT THE CYCLE
- New content → organic test → ad scaling
- Never stop creating while scaling ads
- Creative rotation is critical

KEY METRIC: If it doesn't work organically, it won't work as an ad.
```

## Scripts & Templates

### Script 1: The Instant Response Template (Automated)

```
Subject: Thanks for reaching out, [Name]!

Hi [First Name],

Just saw your message come in. I personally respond to everyone, but wanted to acknowledge you right away.

A few quick questions so I can point you in the right direction:

1. What's your biggest challenge with [their stated problem]?
2. What's your goal for [outcome they want]?
3. What's tried so far?

I'll have specific recommendations ready for you.

If you want to jump on a quick call, I have availability [link to calendar].

Either way, you'll hear from me within 24 hours.

Talk soon,
[Your Name]

P.S. If you're ready to get started now, you can [direct link to purchase/sign up].
```

### Script 2: The Cold Outreach First Message

```
Subject: [Specific observation about them]

Hi [Name],

I noticed [specific thing about their business/content/product].

Most people in [their space] struggle with [problem]. We've helped [similar companies] achieve [specific result].

Quick question: Is [problem] something you're actively trying to solve right now?

If yes, I'd love to show you how we've done it for others.

If not, no worries. Just wanted to reach out.

[Your Name]
```

### Script 3: The Warm Referral Introduction

```
Hi [Referrer Name],

Thanks for connecting us. I really appreciate it.

[Lead Name], I've heard a bit about your situation from [Referrer] and it sounds like [specific challenge].

I'm confident we can help with [specific outcome].

Would you be open to a 20-minute call this week? I have [times available].

If not a fit, I'll tell you honestly. Either way, [Referrer] thought it was worth us connecting, so I wanted to reach out.

Looking forward to talking soon.
```

### Script 4: The "Did I Lose You?" Follow-Up

```
Subject: Checking in

Hi [Name],

I sent you a message a few [days/weeks] ago about [topic].

Just wanted to make sure it didn't get lost.

If you're still interested, I'd love to [specific value]. Here are times I'm available: [calendar link].

If you've moved on or found another solution, no hard feelings. Just wanted to follow up.

Best,
[Your Name]

P.S. If you're ever in a position where [original problem] comes up again, I'm here."
```

## Real Examples

### Example 1: The Warm Process Mistake
A real estate agent used her warm referral scripts on Zillow leads. Charming, relationship-building, soft close. Close rate: 8%. Switched to direct value scripts (cold process): "Here's what's on market, here's the price, want to see it?" Close rate: 34%. Same agent, same market, different process.

### Example 2: The 5-Minute Response Win
A SaaS company had average response time of 47 minutes. Implemented AI chatbot that responded in 45 seconds. Connected rate: from 4% to 31%. Revenue increased 6x. The response time was worth more than any other optimization.

### Example 3: The Content-First Ad Strategy
A coach spent $30K on ads with $0.80 ROAS. Then: Posted organically for 60 days. Found that "The 3 lies coaches tell themselves" got 10x engagement. Ran that exact content as an ad. ROAS: $4.20. Same targeting, proven message. The content proved the ad worked before paying to scale.

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

1. **Don't use warm processes on cold leads** - You'll only catch the easy yeses and miss everyone else.

2. **Don't let response time exceed 5 minutes** - Every minute past 5 kills your chance by 10%.

3. **Don't cold pitch without warming up first** - Cold doesn't mean rude. It means direct value, no fluff.

4. **Don't run ads before testing organically** - If content doesn't work organically, ads won't either.

5. **Don't automate away the human touch entirely** - AI gets them in the door; humans close the deal.

6. **Don't forget to follow up** - Most leads are lost to "no response" not "no interest."

7. **Don't acquire leads you can't serve** - Better to have fewer high-quality leads than many that churn.
