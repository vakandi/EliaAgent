# Pricing Secrets Skill

## Description
Stop leaving money on the table. This skill reveals Alex Hormozi's pricing frameworks, the RAISE letter method, and when to raise prices without losing customers. Learn to price based on value delivered, not time spent.

## Core Principles

### 1. High Close Rate = Underpriced
> "If your close rate is 80%+, you're underpriced by 3-4X."

Your close rate is a pricing signal. Close too many and you're discounting by default.

### 2. No Universal Right Answer
> "There's no right answer, it's going to be what you want to do."

Pricing is strategic. What do you want: more customers or more margin? Both is ideal, but the path differs.

### 3. Move Upstream, Raise Prices
> "If you're moving upmarket, match it with price."

Positioning and pricing must align. Premium positioning without premium pricing destroys credibility.

### 4. Selective Loss is Healthy
> "Raise prices, you won't lose all customers - you'll lose some and replace with better ones."

You don't want every customer. You want the RIGHT customers. Higher prices filter for commitment and value alignment.

### 5. Fractal Pricing Requires Distance
> "Fractal pricing needs to be 5X difference, not 20%."

If you offer 3 tiers, they must be meaningfully different. 20% gaps create decision paralysis. 5x gaps create clear choice.

### 6. RAISE Before You Raise
> "RAISE letter framework: Remind, Address, Invest, Soften, Explain."

Communicate price changes with a framework that preserves relationships.

## Actionable Frameworks

### Framework 1: The RAISE Letter Method

Use this template when communicating price increases to existing customers:

**R - Remind them what they've received**
```
"Over the past [time period], you've received [specific outcomes]:
- [Result 1]
- [Result 2]  
- [Result 3]"
```

**A - Address why things are changing**
```
"As you know, [supply chain costs / market conditions / our investment in better service] have shifted."
```

**I - Invest in their future**
```
"To continue delivering these results and [new benefit], we're making an adjustment."
```

**S - Soften with options**
```
"You have two choices:
A) Lock in your current rate through [date] with [terms]
B) Transition to new pricing starting [date]"
```

**E - Explain the value gap**
```
"The alternative is [competitor], which charges [X] for [less/basically same]. We're confident we're delivering [specific value] at a fair price."
```

### Framework 2: The 5X Fractal Pricing Model

```
TIER 1: ENTRY        $XXX/month
├── Basic access
├── Self-service
└── Community support

TIER 2: PROFESSIONAL $XXX x 5 = $X,XXX/month  
├── Everything in Entry
├── [Premium feature 1]
├── [Premium feature 2]
└── Priority support

TIER 3: ENTERPRISE  $XXX x 5 = $XX,XXX/month
├── Everything in Professional
├── White-glove onboarding
├── Custom integrations
├── Dedicated account manager
└── SLA guarantees
```

**Key rule:** Each tier should feel like a "no brainer upgrade" from the previous. If it doesn't, the gap isn't big enough.

### Framework 3: The Price Testing Ladder

```
STEP 1: Current price - establish baseline close rate
STEP 2: +25% - test elasticity
STEP 3: +50% - test positioning  
STEP 4: +100% - test premium positioning
STEP 5: Find the sweet spot (usually 50-100% above original)
```

## Scripts & Templates

### Script 1: The Price Increase Announcement

```
Subject: Important Update: [Product] Pricing Changes

Hi [Name],

I want to be direct with you about something.

Starting [DATE], [Product] will be increasing prices from $[OLD] to $[NEW] per [month/year].

Here's what you need to know:

WHAT'S CHANGING: Price increase of [X]%

WHAT'S NOT CHANGING: Your current rate is locked in until [DATE] if you [action by DATE].

YOUR OPTIONS:
1. [Lock in current rate] - Save [X]% by acting before [DATE]
2. [Pro-rate option] - Get [X] months at current rate before transitioning
3. [No action needed] - We'll handle the transition smoothly

WHY NOW: [Genuine reason - costs, investment in quality, market conditions]

I know price changes aren't fun. But I also know the value you've gotten from [Product], and I believe this positions us to deliver even more.

Questions? Just reply to this email.

Thanks for being a part of this,
[Your Name]
```

### Script 2: The "That's Too Expensive" Response

```
"I hear you. Price is always a factor.

Let me ask you a couple questions:

First - if price wasn't a concern, would this solve your problem? [Wait for yes]

Second - have you looked at what it costs to [NOT solve this problem / use the alternative / continue status quo]?

[If they haven't] Most people find that the cost of the problem is 3-10x the cost of the solution over [time period].

Here's what I can do: [Offer payment plan / reduced scope / value-add that makes it work].

The question isn't whether you can afford it. The question is whether you can afford NOT to."
```

### Script 3: The "I Need to Talk to My Partner/Boss" Close

```
"Completely understand - smart decision to get buy-in.

Can I ask: What do you think they'll want to know?

[Listen carefully and address those specific concerns]

Here's what I'd recommend: Come to them with not just my price, but my price compared to [alternatives].

I've put together a one-page summary that makes this really easy to present. Want me to send that over?

And here's a question for you: If they approve, will you be the one making the final call? Because if so, I want to make sure YOU have everything you need to feel confident."
```

## Real Examples

### Example 1: The SaaS That 4x'd Prices and Doubled Revenue
A B2B tool at $49/month had 85% close rate. Raised to $197/month. Lost 60% of leads but revenue per customer increased 4x. Most importantly: customer complaints dropped to near zero because higher-priced customers were more committed to success.

### Example 2: The Service Business Fractal Structure
A marketing agency offered:
- Starter: $2,000/mo (basic)
- Growth: $10,000/mo (5x - obviously different)
- Enterprise: $50,000/mo (5x again)

After restructuring from $2,000/$2,500/$3,000, close rate on Growth tier jumped from 12% to 47% because the gap made it feel like a steal.

### Example 3: The RAISE Letter That Preserved Relationships
A software company raised prices 40%. Sent RAISE letter to all customers. Churn: 8%. Previous price increases without framework: 23% churn. One customer replied: "I appreciate the transparency. Keep doing what you're doing."

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

1. **Don't raise prices without a reason** - Always anchor to value delivered or market changes.

2. **Don't raise prices for existing customers without warning** - Use the RAISE framework. They deserve notice.

3. **Don't price tiers too close together** - 20% differences create confusion, not choice.

4. **Don't equate lower price with more sales** - Sometimes higher prices filter for better customers who actually succeed.

5. **Don't fear losing customers to price increases** - You can't serve everyone. Better customers = better results = better referrals.

6. **Don't discount without getting something in return** - Always trade. Extended contract, upfront payment, referral commitment, testimonial.

7. **Don't price based on your costs** - Price based on value delivered. Your costs are irrelevant to the customer.
