# Marketing Strategy Skill

## Description
Build a content engine that attracts your ideal customers on autopilot. This skill covers Alex Hormozi's content repurposing system (21x output), cross-platform distribution, and how to make content that converts viewers into buyers.

## Core Principles

### 1. Audience Alignment
> "Make content about the stuff that the type of people you want to attract make."

Your content should be a signal that filters FOR your ideal customer and filters OUT everyone else.

### 2. Value Density
> "Double down on highest value per second for that audience."

Every piece of content should deliver maximum value in minimum time. If it bores you to make it, it bores them to watch it.

### 3. Buyer-Focused Content
> "The core meat should be focused on what people who are buying want."

Content for awareness is different from content for conversion. Most creators make awareness content forever. Make content your buyers want to see.

### 4. Cross-Platform Everything
> "Cross-platform everything - reels to TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Facebook, LinkedIn, X."

One piece of content should live everywhere. Adapt, don't recreate.

### 5. The 21x Output System
> "Take transcriptions → posts → infographics = 21X output."

One long-form piece becomes 21 distinct pieces. This is the content multiplication method.

### 6. Ads and Content Are the Same
> "The algorithm for ads and content are the same."

What works organically will work as paid. Test with content first, scale with ads.

## Actionable Frameworks

```
ONE SOURCE PIECE (Long-form video/podcast)
│
├── TRANSCRIPTION (Word-for-word text)
│   │
│   ├── 3x SHORT-FORM VIDEOS (30-60 sec clips)
│   │   ├── Hook clip
│   │   ├── Value clip
│   │   └── CTA clip
│   │
│   ├── 7x LINKEDIN POSTS (Long-form text posts)
│   │   ├── Key insight 1
│   │   ├── Key insight 2
│   │   ├── Key insight 3
│   │   ├── Story/example post
│   │   ├── Controversial take
│   │   ├── How-to post
│   │   └── Quote card post
│   │
│   ├── 5x THREADS (Twitter/X threads)
│   │   ├── Main topic thread
│   │   ├── Step-by-step thread
│   │   ├── Myth-busting thread
│   │   ├── Case study thread
│   │   └── Q&A thread
│   │
│   ├── 3x EMAIL SEQUENCES (Nurture content)
│   │   ├── The problem email
│   │   ├── The solution email
│   │   └── The transformation email
│   │
│   └── 3x INFOGRAPHICS (Visual content)
│       ├── Framework infographic
│       ├── Statistics/data graphic
│       └── Process/steps graphic
│
TOTAL: 21 PIECES OF CONTENT
```

### Framework 2: The Buyer-Attracting Content Framework

```
FOR EACH PIECE OF CONTENT, ANSWER:

1. WHO is this for? (Be specific - job title, company size, challenge)
2. WHAT do they want? (Outcome they desire)
3. WHY do they want it? (Emotional driver)
4. HOW will this content help them?
5. WHERE are they in their journey? (Aware/unaware)

CONTENT TYPES BY STAGE:

AWARENESS (Cold audience):
- Stories about transformation
- Industry pain points
- "What most people get wrong about X"
- Personal journey

CONSIDERATION (Warm audience):
- How-to guides
- Framework breakdowns
- Case studies
- Comparisons

DECISION (Hot audience):
- Testimonials
- Behind-the-scenes
- Offer details
- Live demonstrations
- Urgency content
```

## Scripts & Templates

### Script 1: The Hook Formula (For Short-Form Video)

```
PICK ONE STRUCTURE:

STRUCTURE A: The Contrast Hook
"This is why [common belief] is actually [opposite truth].
Most people think [X]. The data shows [Y].
Here's what nobody talks about..."

STRUCTURE B: The Pattern Interrupt
"Stop [common mistake]. 
I've watched [100s/thousands] of [type of people] and [X]% do this same thing.
The problem is..."

STRUCTURE C: The Story Hook  
"Two years ago I [did something stupid].
I thought [wrong belief].
Then [what happened] changed everything."

STRUCTURE D: The Framework Hook
"The [X] framework that [achieved result]:
Step 1: [Counterintuitive action]
Step 2: [What most people skip]
Step 3: [The unexpected element]"

STRUCTURE E: The Stat Hook
"[X]% of [professionals/people] will [negative outcome].
The reason isn't what you think.
It's actually [simple explanation]."
```

## Real Examples

### Example 1: The One Video, 47-Piece System
A fitness coach recorded one 45-minute training video. Transcribed it into:
- 3 YouTube Shorts (tips)
- 7 Instagram Reels (exercises)
- 5 TikToks (transformation story)
- 7 LinkedIn posts (business angle)
- 10 Twitter posts (actionable tips)
- 5 infographics (meal plans)
- 3 email sequences (nurture)
- 1 long-form YouTube (full workout)
- 1 blog post (SEO)

Total: 47 pieces from 1 hour of content creation.

### Example 2: The Buyer-Focused Pivot
A consultant made "thought leadership" about entrepreneurship. Posts got likes but no customers. Switched to "what buyers want to know before they hire":
- "Questions to ask any consultant before hiring"
- "Red flags in our industry"
- "What our onboarding process looks like"

Leads increased 3x. The content filtered for serious buyers.

### Example 3: The Quality-First Ad Strategy
A software company spent $50K on ads that flopped. Then: Made 20 pieces of organic content. Found the 3 that performed best organically. Ran THOSE as ads. ROAS went from 0.8x to 4.2x. Same targeting, better creative.

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

1. **Don't create for everyone** - Broad content attracts no one. Specificity attracts superfans.

2. **Don't skip the transcription step** - This is where most of your 21x output comes from.

3. **Don't repurpose without adapting** - Each platform has different norms. Don't just upload the same video everywhere.

4. **Don't make awareness content forever** - At some point, start selling. Content without conversion is vanity.

5. **Don't ignore what performs organically** - Your best organic content is your best ad creative.

6. **Don't post without a system** - Batch create, schedule, and automate. Consistency beats perfection.

7. **Don't make content you wouldn't watch** - If it's boring to make, it's boring to watch.
