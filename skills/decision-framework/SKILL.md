# Decision Framework Skill

## Description
Make better decisions faster with Alex Hormozi's battle-tested frameworks. Cut analysis paralysis, manage risk intelligently, and know when to pivot vs. persist using the upside/downside method and regret minimization.

## Core Principles

### 1. The Upside/Downside Filter
> "What's the upside? What's the downside? Can I live with the downside?"

Every decision reduced to three questions. If the downside is survivable, the upside is worth pursuing.

### 2. Decision Speed vs. Data Gathering
> "If you can make the decision, fail, and go back in less time than gathering data, then make the failure in judgment by not deciding."

Sometimes the cost of delay exceeds the cost of making the wrong call.

### 3. Focus is Elimination
> "Focus is the elimination of alternatives."

Every "yes" to something is a "no" to everything else. True focus requires killing good ideas that don't serve your core path.

### 4. The Business You're In Is Temporary
> "The business you're in now is probably not the one you'll end up in, but the lessons you learn are the ones you'll keep."

Make decisions based on transferable skills, not just current business outcomes.

### 5. Regret Minimization
> "Regret minimization framework - imagine 85 year old you."

What would your 85-year-old self wish you had done? Usually the answer is: the scary thing.

### 6. New = Misjudged Risk
> "The risk of new is one of the most misjudged risks in business."

We overweight the risks of trying and underweight the risks of staying.

## Actionable Frameworks

### Framework 1: The Upside/Downside Decision Matrix

```
DECISION: [Write the decision clearly]

═══════════════════════════════════════════
UPTSIDE (Best case scenario):
═══════════════════════════════════════════
What is the best possible outcome?
-

What is the probability of this outcome?
-

What is the value if it happens?
-

═══════════════════════════════════════════
DOWNSIDE (Worst case scenario):
═══════════════════════════════════════════
What is the worst possible outcome?
-

Can I survive this outcome?
YES / NO / PARTIALLY
-

How long to recover?
-

═══════════════════════════════════════════
DECISION:
═══════════════════════════════════════════
Can you live with the downside? YES/NO
Is the upside worth the risk? YES/NO

→ YES/YES = DO IT
→ YES/NO = RETHINK UPSIDE
→ NO/YES = REDUCE DOWNSIDE FIRST
→ NO/NO = DON'T DO IT
```

### Framework 2: The 10/10/10 Regret Minimization

```
QUESTION: Should I [decision]?

10 MINUTES FROM NOW:
How will I feel about this decision?
-

10 MONTHS FROM NOW:
How will this decision have changed my trajectory?
-

10 YEARS FROM NOW:
What will my 85-year-old self wish I had done?
-

SCORING:
If 10-years-you would regret NOT doing it → DO IT
If 10-years-you would regret DOING it → DON'T DO IT
If unclear → The risk of not doing is usually higher
```

### Framework 3: The Pivot vs. Persist Checklist

```
BEFORE PIVOTING, CHECK:

[ ] Have I given this enough time? (Minimum 90 days)
[ ] Have I actually executed the plan, or just planned?
[ ] Is the market the problem, or my execution?
[ ] Do I have evidence, or just feelings?
[ ] Have I talked to 10 customers about why they didn't buy?
[ ] Is the problem solvable with more time/money?

BEFORE PERSISTING, CHECK:

[ ] Is there a clear path to profitability?
[ ] Do I have runway to continue?
[ ] Am I seeing ANY positive signals?
[ ] Is my motivation intrinsic or ego-driven?
[ ] Can I separate short-term pain from long-term gain?

DECISION RULE:
If evidence points to market fit but execution gaps → PERSIST + FIX
If evidence points to no market fit → PIVOT
If evidence is unclear → GATHER MORE (but set a deadline)
```

## Scripts & Templates

### Script 1: The Quick Decision Test

```
When facing a decision, ask yourself:

"If I knew the outcome either way, which would I choose?"

[Usually you already know the answer - you're just avoiding it]

"Am I delaying because I need more information, or because I'm afraid?"

[If afraid → Do it anyway unless downside is catastrophic]
[If need info → What specific info would change your mind? Get that.]

"Will this matter in 5 years?"

[If no → Don't spend more than 5 minutes deciding]
[If yes → Use the full framework above]
```

### Script 2: The "Should I Quit My Job" Framework

```
Step 1: Calculate your floor
- What are your minimum monthly expenses?
- How long can you survive without income?

Step 2: Calculate your runway
- Months of runway = Savings / Monthly burn
- Target: 12-18 months minimum

Step 3: The real question
"Are you doing your current job well?"

[If no] - Quit internally first. Get your performance up.
[If yes] - Then the question is: Is the upside of starting worth leaving stability?

Step 4: The fallback question
"Can I get my current job back if this fails?"

[If yes] - The downside is smaller than it feels
[If no] - Make sure you're not overestimating your runway

Step 5: The regret test
"At 85, will you regret not trying?"
```

### Script 3: The Partnership Decision Guide

```
BEFORE COMMITTING TO A PARTNERSHIP:

The 3 Questions:
1. "Does this person help me become the person I want to become?"
2. "Do they get me closer or further from my goals?"
3. "Can I work with them during the worst 10% of times?"

The Stress Test:
- Can you disagree with them and still respect each other?
- Do they hold the same core values under pressure?
- Will they still show up when things get hard?

The Capacity Check:
"It is significantly harder to be kind when you do not have capacity."

Are BOTH parties in capacity? If one is running on empty, wait.
```

## Real Examples

### Example 1: The Pivot at the Right Time
A founder spent 2 years building a B2C app. Close rate was 3%. Talked to 50 customers who didn't convert. Found they wanted enterprise features. Pivoted to B2B. 18 months later: $2M ARR. The business "changed completely" but the lessons about customer discovery transferred.

### Example 2: The Persist That Paid Off
An e-commerce brand saw revenue drop 40% in 2020. Owner almost quit. Used the framework: Market was down, but unit economics were still positive. Doubled down on content, improved product quality, kept prices up. 2021: Revenue 3x previous high. The "downside" was survivable; they just needed patience.

### Example 3: The Regret Minimization Win
A consultant was offered a partnership at a big firm (safe, high salary) vs. starting his own company (uncertain, high potential). Used the 85-year-old test. Imagined his 85-year-old self at his funeral. "Did I play it safe or did I try?" He started his company. 5 years later: 10x his former salary, and more importantly, zero regret.

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

1. **Don't over-rotate on data when speed matters** - Sometimes a wrong decision made quickly is better than a right decision made too late.

2. **Don't confuse activity with progress** - Planning is not executing. Data gathering can become procrastination.

3. **Don't stay in a bad decision too long** - Cutting losses early is a skill, not a failure.

4. **Don't let fear of failure paralyze you** - "Losers stay losers because they are afraid of losing, so they never try."

5. **Don't confuse pivoting with quitting** - Pivoting is strategic. Quitting is emotional. Know which one you're doing.

6. **Don't make permanent decisions based on temporary feelings** - Fatigue, stress, and frustration distort judgment. Sleep on big decisions.

7. **Don't ignore the downside test** - If you can't live with the downside, no upside justifies the risk.
