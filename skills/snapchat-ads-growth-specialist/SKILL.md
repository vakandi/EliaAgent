# Clothing Ads Specialist Skill

**Skill Name**: clothing-ads-specialist
**Description**: This master skill transforms an AI into a high-performance growth engine specifically for clothing and apparel brands. It merges the Acquisition.com "More-Better-New" framework with advanced Meta/Snapchat "Andromeda" logic, where creative execution replaces traditional manual targeting. The skill focuses on high-velocity creative testing (the 322 and 503 methods) to identify winning "angles" that scale apparel revenue while maintaining a stable LTV:CAC ratio.

---

## I. Core Principles for Apparel Growth

- **Creative is the Targeting**: In the modern algorithm (Andromeda), you no longer "find" your audience; your creative filters for them. If your ad features a specific style of streetwear, the algorithm serves it to people who engage with that style.
- **The Rule of 100 for Fashion**: Success requires 100 primary actions: Analyzing 100 competitor clothing hooks, spending at least $100/day to exit the learning phase, and committing to 100 minutes of creative production (filming/editing) daily.
- **Native over Polished**: For clothing, raw "iPhone-style" content (unboxings, try-on hauls, "get ready with me") consistently outperforms high-production studio shoots on platforms like Snapchat and Reels.
- **The Value Equation in Apparel**: `Value = (Dream Outcome × Likelihood of Success) / (Time Delay × Effort/Sacrifice)`

---

## II. Actionable Frameworks

### 1. The 503 Assembly System (Creative Velocity)

Instead of filming 750 unique videos, film 58 clips and assemble them:
- **50 Hooks** (0-3s): Focus 80% of effort here. For clothing: "Stop buying basic tees," "The only dress you need this summer," or a shock visual of fabric quality.
- **5 "Meats"** (Bodies): Demonstration of fit, fabric close-ups, unboxing, or customer testimonials.
- **3 CTAs**: Direct ("Shop now"), Conditional ("If you want to upgrade your wardrobe, click below"), or Engagement ("Comment 'Style' for the link").

### 2. The 322 Testing Methodology

For every $1k/month in spend, test one new "concept" weekly:
- 3 Creatives (Video, Static Image, Carousel)
- 2 Headlines (Using the MAGIC formula)
- 2 Primary Texts

### 3. The 70/20/10 Budget Allocation

Once a winner is identified:
- **70%** → Current top 2 ads (Winners)
- **20%** → "Kaleidoscope" testing—same winning script but different outfits/backgrounds
- **10%** → Wild experiments with entirely new angles

## III. Scripts & Templates

### The MAGIC Naming Formula (Offer Headlines)
- **Magnet**: "Buttery soft leggings"
- **Avatar**: "For busy moms"
- **Goal**: "That stay up all day"
- **Interval**: "Guaranteed for 12 months"
- **Container**: "The 2026 Collection"

### Apparel Hook Formulas (First 3 Seconds)
- **The Call-out**: "If you're under 5'4" and struggle to find jeans that fit..."
- **The Damaging Admission**: "We aren't the cheapest brand, but we're the only one using 100% sustainable silk"
- **The Comparison Outcome**: "$40 Amazon pants vs. Our $120 Performance Chinos"

---

## IV. Technical Implementation & Optimization

- **Pixel/CAPI Calibration**: Mandatory installation of Snap Pixel or Meta Conversions API to track "Purchase" events.
- **Broad Targeting for Scale**: Use 100% broad targeting (only age/gender) and let the creative find the buyer.
- **The 3-Day Rule**: Never scale a budget or change a creative during the first 3 days.
- **Operational KPI Audit**:
  - Swipe-Up/CTR: Hook health (0-3s)
  - Engagement/Watch Time: Body health (fit/style demonstration)
  - Conversion Rate: Offer/Price health

---

## V. Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

1. **Chasing "Reach" over Efficiency**: Focus on LTV:CAC minimum 3:1 ratio.
2. **Using "Warm" Content on Cold Audiences**: Use direct-value, hook-heavy content for strangers.
3. **Ignoring the "Denominated Friction"**: CTAs should be "Swipe Up to Buy" or "One-Tap Shop."
4. **"Creative Fatigue" Blindness**: Multiply winners (Kaleidoscope strategy) before they burn out.
