# Entrepreneur Core Skill

## Description
Build the inner game that drives outer success. This skill covers Alex Hormozi's wisdom on relationships, capacity, purpose, and the personal foundations that make business success sustainable. "Seek abundance so you can have spillover and help other people."

## Core Principles

### 1. People Over Everything
> "Does this person help me become the person I want to become? Do they get me closer or further?"

Every relationship should pass this test. Business partnerships, friendships, team members.

### 2. Capacity Enables Kindness
> "It is significantly harder to be kind when you do not have capacity."

Build capacity first. Then you can afford to be generous. Empty tanks cannot pour.

### 3. Abundance Enables Spillover
> "Seek abundance so you can have spillover and help other people."

The goal of wealth is not consumption. It's the ability to help others at scale.

### 4. Your Spouse Marries Two People
> "Your spouse marries two people - the person you are and the person you're gonna become. Make sure they love both."

Choose a partner who believes in your future self, not just your current self.

### 5. The Right Partner Changes Everything
> "If you find a girl who believes in your dreams more than you do, who makes you wanna be a better man, just marry her."

Find someone who elevates you. The right partner is a multiplier on everything.

### 6. Business is Personal
> "The business you're in now is probably not the one you'll end up in, but the lessons you learn are the ones you'll keep."

Business success comes from personal growth. Work on yourself to improve your business.

## Actionable Frameworks

### Framework 1: The Relationship Filter

```
FOR EVERY RELATIONSHIP, ASSESS:

1. DOES THIS HELP ME BECOME?
   - Better version of myself?
   - More skilled?
   - More grounded?
   
   YES → Keep/strengthen
   NO → Evaluate if worth keeping

2. DOES THIS GET ME CLOSER OR FURTHER?
   - From my goals?
   - From who I want to become?
   - From the life I want?
   
   CLOSER → Invest in this relationship
   FURTHER → Distance or exit

3. IS THIS RECIPROCAL?
   - Do I add value to them?
   - Do they add value to me?
   - Is there mutual respect?
   
   YES → Healthy relationship
   NO → Evaluate sustainability

RELATIONSHIP TYPES:

FAMILY:
- Non-negotiable investment
- Schedule protected time
- Set boundaries, not walls

PARTNERS:
- Shared vision required
- Complementary skills
- Aligned on values

TEAM:
- High bar for entry
- Culture fit
- Performance standards

MENTORS:
- Seek主动ly
- Reciprocate value
- Pay it forward later

PEERS:
- Challenge each other
- Share learnings
- Celebrate wins
```

### Framework 2: The Capacity Building System

```
CAPACITY = TIME + ENERGY + MONEY + SKILLS

TIME CAPACITY:
├── Identify time drains
├── Automate/eliminate non-essentials
├── Protect deep work blocks
└── Schedule recovery

ENERGY CAPACITY:
├── Sleep (7-8 hours minimum)
├── Exercise (3-5x weekly)
├── Nutrition (fuel, not comfort)
└── Mental recovery (breaks, vacations)

MONEY CAPACITY:
├── Reduce unnecessary expenses
├── Build emergency fund (6 months)
├── Invest in tools that save time
└── Pay yourself last (initially)

SKILL CAPACITY:
├── Identify gaps
├── Invest in learning
├── Hire for weaknesses
└── Delegate what others do better

CAPACITY CHECK:
Are you running on empty?
→ YES: Build capacity before taking on more
→ NO: You're ready to grow

REMEMBER: "It is significantly harder to be kind when you do not have capacity."
Build the tank before trying to pour from it.
```

### Framework 3: The Life-Business Integration

```
GOAL: Build a business that supports your life,
not a life that supports your business.

STEP 1: DEFINE YOUR IDEAL LIFE
- What does a perfect day look like?
- What do you want your relationships to be?
- What health/fitness goals matter?
- What experiences do you want to have?
- How much money do you need for that life?

STEP 2: DESIGN BUSINESS AROUND LIFE
- What hours do you want to work?
- What kind of work energizes you?
- What can you automate or delegate?
- What timeline is realistic?

STEP 3: SET BOUNDARIES
- Non-negotiable time blocks
- Relationship protection rules
- Health commitments
- Exit strategy when boundaries blur

STEP 4: REGULAR REVIEW
- Monthly: Am I living my ideal week?
- Quarterly: Am I moving toward my ideal life?
- Annually: Big picture reassessment

THE TEST:
If your business success requires sacrificing the life you want,
you're not successful. You're just busy.

"Seek abundance so you can have spillover and help other people."
Abundance means enough for your life AND to help others.
```

## Scripts & Templates

### Script 1: The "Should I Take This Meeting/Opportunity" Test

```
Ask yourself:

1. "Does this person help me become the person I want to become?"
   - YES → Strong yes
   - UNSURE → Proceed with caution
   - NO → Decline politely

2. "Does this get me closer or further from my goals?"
   - CLOSER → Consider saying yes
   - FURTHER → Decline
   - UNRELATED → Depends on other factors

3. "Do I have capacity for this without compromising existing commitments?"
   - YES → Consider yes
   - NO → Decline or negotiate timing

4. "Would my spouse/family support this?"
   - If no → This is a warning sign
   - If yes → Proceed with confidence

RESPONSE TEMPLATES:

DECLINING:
"Thanks for thinking of me, but I need to pass on this one.
I need to stay focused on [current priority].
Let's stay in touch for future opportunities."

ACCEPTING:
"I'm in. Here's what I need to make this work: [boundaries].
Looking forward to it."

NEGOTIATING:
"I'd love to do this. Can we adjust [aspect] to make it work better for my schedule?"
```

### Script 2: The Pre-Relationship Questions

```
FOR BUSINESS PARTNERSHIPS:

1. "Have you worked with this person during hard times?"
   (People show true colors under pressure)

2. "Do they share my core values?"
   (Values align under stress)

3. "Can I see us both winning?"
   (If only one can win, avoid)

4. "What happens if this fails?"
   (Understand downside alignment)

5. "Will they tell me hard truths?"
   (Candor > comfort)

FOR PERSONAL RELATIONSHIPS:

1. "Do they believe in who I'm becoming?"
   (Not just who I am)

2. "Do they make me want to be better?"
   (Elevation test)

3. "Can I be honest with them always?"
   (Trust test)

4. "Are they my equal in some dimension?"
   (Respect requires parity)

5. "Would I want my kids to be like them?"
   (Character matters most)
```

### Script 3: The Capacity Audit

```
DAILY CHECK-IN:

Energy Level: [1-10]
Sleep Quality: [1-10]
Stress Level: [1-10]
Time Available: [More than enough / Just enough / Not enough]

IF ANY ARE LOW:
- What's draining me?
- What's restoring me?
- What can I eliminate today?

WEEKLY REVIEW:

Relationships: [Invested / Neglected]
Health: [Prioritized / Ignored]
Business: [Growing / Stagnant]
Personal: [Growing / Stagnant]

IF IMBALANCED:
- Which area am I sacrificing?
- Why am I sacrificing it?
- Is the sacrifice temporary or permanent?

ANNUAL BUCKET LIST:
[ ] Family priorities met?
[ ] Health goals achieved?
[ ] Personal growth made?
[ ] Business milestones hit?
[ ] Financial targets reached?

IF ANY NEGLECTED:
- Why?
- What needs to change?
- Who can help?

"It is significantly harder to be kind when you do not have capacity."
Refill the tank first.
```

## Real Examples

### Example 1: The "Right Partner" Multiplier
A founder was struggling in his business and marriage simultaneously. Wife challenged him to be better, pushed him to do the hard work, believed in his potential when he doubted himself. He credited her belief as the reason he didn't quit. 5 years later: successful business, stronger marriage, two kids. "Make sure they love both" (current and future self).

### Example 2: The Capacity Before Generosity
An entrepreneur was burnt out, snapping at team, ignoring family. Decided to build capacity first: hired VA to handle admin, blocked Saturday for family, started exercising. First month: felt guilty. Third month: teams performance improved, family felt his presence, revenue increased. "You cannot pour from an empty cup."

### Example 3: The Relationship Filter Saves Everything
A founder was about to partner with a "successful" person. Ran him through the filter: Does he help me become? (Sometimes). Closer or further? (Mixed). Reciprocal? (Uneven). Chose not to partner. That person had a public blowup 6 months later with another partner. The filter worked.

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

1. **Don't sacrifice relationships for business** - No amount of success replaces broken relationships.

2. **Don't ignore the "capacity" warning** - If you're running on empty, you're one mistake away from breakdown.

3. **Don't partner with people who don't believe in your future** - Only people who love both selves will stay through the hard times.

4. **Don't let business consume your life** - "The business you're in now" is temporary. Your life is not.

5. **Don't skip the capacity audit** - Regular check-ins prevent slow erosion.

6. **Don't mistake being busy for being effective** - Building the right thing matters more than building everything.

7. **Don't forget the "spillover"** - The goal of abundance is to help others. Keep that in mind.
