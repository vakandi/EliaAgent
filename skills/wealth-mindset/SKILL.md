# Wealth Mindset Skill

## Description
Develop the mental frameworks and long-term thinking that create generational wealth. This skill covers Alex Hormozi's insights on compounding, the value of stories, paying to win, and why "the next five years are gonna be nuts" with AI and cloud computing creating the next boom of billionaires.

## Core Principles

### 1. Compounding Is Everything
> "Many of you probably remember when you were getting into this business, you tried a bunch of stuff, most of it didn't work and then something started to work."

Success is not linear. It's flat, then exponential. Most people quit right before the curve.

### 2. More Is the Highest Risk-Adjusted Move
> "More is the highest risk adjusted move that you can do."

When you find something that works, do more of it. The highest risk is not doing enough of what works.

### 3. The Value of the Story
> "The value of the story - willingness to lose battles to win wars."

Short-term losses for long-term positioning. Not every battle matters. Choose your wars.

### 4. Pay to Be Number One
> "It's willing to pay to be able to say you're number one."

Positioning has value beyond the product. Being #1 in a category is worth more than being #15 with a better product.

### 5. The Next Boom Is Now
> "The next five years are gonna be nuts - 25 years ago cloud computing came out and all the billionaires were made. The next boom is this."

AI and digital infrastructure are creating the next generation of wealth. This is the moment.

### 6. Seek Abundance to Help Others
> "Seek abundance so you can have spillover and help other people."

Wealth is not the goal. It's the tool. The goal is freedom to help others.

## Actionable Frameworks

### Framework 1: The Compounding Framework

```
YEAR 1: LEARNING
- Most experiments fail
- Revenue: unpredictable
- Focus: Find what works
- Mental state: Doubt

YEAR 2: PROOF
- Something starts working
- Revenue: Starts to stabilize
- Focus: Double down on what works
- Mental state: Cautious optimism

YEAR 3: COMPOUNDING
- What worked gets better
- Revenue: Accelerates
- Focus: Scale what works
- Mental state: Confidence

YEAR 4-5: EXPLOSION
- Compounding kicks in
- Revenue: 10x previous
- Focus: Systems and leverage
- Mental state: Momentum

YEAR 5-10: ABUNDANCE
- Everything compounds
- Revenue: Life-changing
- Focus: Legacy and giving
- Mental state: Purpose

KEY INSIGHT: Most people quit in Year 1-2. The curve is steepest right before it takes off.
```

### Framework 2: The "More" Decision Matrix

```
WHEN SOMETHING WORKS, ASK:

1. CAN I DO MORE OF THIS?
   [ ] More volume (same thing)
   [ ] More frequency (repeat customers)
   [ ] More channels (same audience, different places)
   [ ] More adjacent offers (existing customers, new products)

2. IS MORE THE RIGHT ANSWER?
   - If working at small scale = YES, do more
   - If not working at small scale = NO, fix before scaling

3. WHAT'S THE RISK OF NOT DOING MORE?
   - Competitor will take share
   - Opportunity window will close
   - Revenue left on table

4. WHAT'S THE RISK OF DOING MORE?
   - Usually lower than it feels
   - Can always pull back
   - Mistakes at scale are usually recoverable

DECISION: If it works, do more. If it doesn't work, fix it. Don't do something new until the current thing is maximized.
```

### Framework 3: The Wealth Building Timeline

```
DECADE 1: EARN (25-35)
- Primary focus: Build skills and income
- Secondary: Keep expenses low
- Goal: Save first $100K
- Mindset: Learn, not earn

DECADE 2: MULTIPLY (35-45)
- Primary focus: Scale what works
- Secondary: Invest in systems
- Goal: $1M+ net worth
- Mindset: Leverage, not labor

DECADE 3: PROTECT (45-55)
- Primary focus: Preserve and grow
- Secondary: Tax optimization
- Goal: Multi-millionaire
- Mindset: Protect and compound

DECADE 4: TRANSFER (55-65)
- Primary focus: Legacy planning
- Secondary: Philanthropy
- Goal: Generational wealth
- Mindset: Give, not just keep

KEY: Each decade has different priorities. Don't confuse them.
```

## Scripts & Templates

### Script 1: The "Should I Quit My Business" Test

```
Ask yourself:

1. "Is this business still growing?"
   - Yes, slowly but steadily: Keep going
   - Yes, rapidly: Double down
   - No, plateaued: Diagnose why
   - Declining: Consider exit

2. "Am I the right person to run this?"
   - Yes: Keep going
   - No: Hire/partner or pivot

3. "Is the problem the business or me?"
   - If business model broken: Pivot or exit
   - If leadership broken: Fix yourself
   - If market timing: Wait or pivot

4. "What's the opportunity cost?"
   - What else could I build in the same time?
   - Is this the best use of my time?

5. "What would I tell my 85-year-old self?"
   - Usually: Try harder
   - Sometimes: Let go

DECISION: Usually stay and push through. But be honest about which situation you're in.
```

### Script 2: The Abundance Mindset Reframe

```
WHEN FACING COMPETITION:
"Competitors validate the market and split the work.
I don't need everyone. I need my people."

WHEN FACING FAILURE:
"Every failure is a tuition payment toward the lesson
that will make me wealthy."

WHEN FACING DOUBT:
"Most people remember when they got into this,
most of it didn't work, then something started to work.
I'm in the phase where most of it doesn't work.
That's normal. That's the tuition."

WHEN FACING JEALOUSY:
"[Other person's] success doesn't diminish mine.
There's enough for everyone who executes."
```

### Script 3: The "Pay to Win" Decision

```
SITUATION: Should I pay for [positioning/advantage]?

QUESTION: If I could buy the #1 position in this category,
how much would it be worth?

[Estimate the lifetime value of being #1]

IS THE INVESTMENT LESS THAN THE VALUE?
YES → Pay to win
NO → Find another way to differentiate

EXAMPLE:
- $10K to sponsor event and get "sponsored by #1" status
- Value: $100K over 5 years from positioning
- Decision: Pay the $10K
```

## Real Examples

### Example 1: The Compounding Wait
An e-commerce founder had flat revenue for 18 months. Almost quit. Then: 3 products went viral simultaneously. Revenue 10x in 6 months. The 18 months of building audience, testing products, and refining systems was the investment. The explosion was the return. The curve looked flat right before it went vertical.

### Example 2: The "Do More" Win
A consultant was making $8K/month. Identified his best service: high-ticket coaching. Doubled down: Raised prices, stopped doing other services, focused 100% on coaching. Revenue: $25K/month in 6 months. Same skills, more focus.

### Example 3: The Pay to Win Play
A new SaaS entered a crowded market. Instead of competing on product, they sponsored the industry's biggest conference and got "Official Sponsor" status. Paid $50K. Booked $400K in first year from the positioning. Being associated with the category leader (as sponsor) gave them credibility they couldn't buy any other way.

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

1. **Don't quit right before the compounding kicks in** - The curve looks flat before it goes vertical. Stay long enough to see it.

2. **Don't do more of what doesn't work** - "More" only applies to what works. Fix broken things before scaling them.

3. **Don't compare your chapter 1 to someone else's chapter 20** - Compounding takes time. Their wealth took decades.

4. **Don't let competition make you second-guess winning** - Focus on your customers, not competitors.

5. **Don't undervalue positioning** - Being #1 in a category is worth more than having a better product as #2.

6. **Don't save instead of invest** - Money sitting is losing to inflation. Money working compounds.

7. **Don't forget the "why"** - Wealth is a tool, not a destination. Keep the purpose in mind.
